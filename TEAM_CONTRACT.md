6.3 PR review workflow
Every PR to main requires 2 teammate approvals before it merges. This is enforced by branch protection on main — set at Team Prepwork §4 Step 7. Not 1 approval, not 3. Two.
PR author does not merge their own PR. Two OTHER teammates approve; either the author or a reviewer clicks Merge after the second approval lands.
Stale-approval dismissal is on. If you push new commits to a PR after an approval, the approval resets and teammates re-review the newer code.
PR title format: <slug>: <short imperative summary> — for example, feature/eval-harness: add held-out set loader.
PR body includes: (a) what changed, (b) any teammate action needed, (c) test evidence (screenshot or paste of pytest -v).
PRs auto-link to your GitHub Project board — opening a PR moves the linked card to In review; merging moves it to Done.
No bypass exists. Branch protection is enabled with “Do not allow bypassing.” Teams of 4 always have 3 teammates other than the author available to review, so 2 approvals is achievable when the team is coordinating well. If a real emergency requires temporarily modifying the rule, open a thread in the course Slack channel naming the specific circumstance; the Lead Instructor decides whether to authorize a policy exception. Restore the rule immediately after any authorized exception. Do not modify or disable branch protection unilaterally.
